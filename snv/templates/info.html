What is the added value of SNVdb?

SNVdb is a novel biological database aiming to bridge the gap between a number of databases with different types of information.

This site allows the user to display human single nucleotide variations (SNVs) onto the three-dimensional structure of the proteins they map to, showing whether these variations can be a disrupting factor in the interaction with other proteins (i.e. they map to a residue involved in the interaction interface between the two interacting parners). The inclusion of disease association data about the SNVs allows the user to make informed hypothesis about the causal mechanism of these disorders

Where does all this information come from?

The main sources of information on which we rely are:

  • Sequence databases:
    • Uniprot
    • NCBI/Gene
    • dbSNP
  • Protein domains and families databases:
    • Pfam
  • Disease databases:
    • OMIM
  • Protein-protein interaction databases:
    • 3did
  • Protein structure databases:
    • Protein Data Bank

What is the point of such a website?

By making this information publicly available, especially to researchers in the biomedical sciences field, our goal is to help targeting important proteins, interactions and SNVs underpinning human diseases, in order to facilitate the opening of new research avenues that can bring further understanding of the causes behind them.
